EVERY TECHNIQUE IS PUBLISHED RESEARCH

Not vibes. Literature.

Each prompting technique cites a specific paper or framework — grounding design decisions in evidence.

1
COSTAR-A Framework
arXiv 2510.12637 (2025)
Adaptive prompt framework — Context, Objective, Style, Tone, Audience, Response + Answer. Ensures completeness and output shape control.
2
Multi-Agent Debate
Du, Li, Torralba, Tenenbaum, Mordatch — 2023
Two agents debate internally before responding. Shown to improve factuality and reasoning — applied to our Weekly Report.
3
Metacognitive Prompting
Mazari — 2025
Activates self-reflection in both model and user. We use it for reflective questions + the "L (Learned)" extension.
4
Chain-of-Thought
Wei et al. — NeurIPS 2022
Step-by-step internal reasoning. Our Prompt 1 uses an explicit 4-step chain before producing output.
5
Few-Shot Learning
Brown et al. — "Language Models are Few-Shot Learners" — NeurIPS 2020
Provide 2-3 grounded examples so the model mirrors the target format. Critical for STAR(L) auto-structuring.
6
STAR(L) — Original Extension
Han, S. — 2026 (this project)
Classic STAR + "L" for Learned. Turns interview structuring into a metacognitive growth tool.
